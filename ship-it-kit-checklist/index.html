1. Auth and access

  • ✓Login, signup, reset flow: every basic path works without dead ends.
  • ✓Protected routes: dashboard and billing pages cannot leak to signed-out users.
  • ✓User ownership: every project, row, and mutation respects the signed-in user.

2. Billing and money

  • ✓Checkout flow: customer can actually pay without broken redirects or missing prices.
  • ✓Portal access: billing settings let users manage plan and card details.
  • ✓Webhook handling: payment success, cancel, and plan changes update your app state.

3. Product basics

  • ✓One clear first action: a new user knows what to do after signup.
  • ✓Empty states: blank dashboard views explain the next step instead of looking broken.
  • ✓Settings page: profile, billing, and account basics exist before launch.

4. Email and delivery

  • ✓Transactional email: welcome, reset, and billing flows have the right emails wired.
  • ✓Sender clarity: receipts and onboarding emails look trustworthy, not hacked together.
  • ✓Post-purchase path: buyers know where to go immediately after paying.

5. Launch hygiene

  • ✓Meta tags and OG image: your product shares cleanly on social and chat.
  • ✓Error handling: failures show human messages, not raw stack traces.
  • ✓Fast deploy: one repo, one env file, one path to production.

6. Founder reality check

  • ✓Can you demo it in 2 minutes? If not, simplify the story.
  • ✓Can you explain the value in one sentence? If not, sharpen positioning first.
  • ✓Are you building product work, not plumbing? That is the real point of using a starter.
