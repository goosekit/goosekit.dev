Stripe + Supabase invoice reminder debug

Stripe invoice paid, but reminder emails keep sending?

This is an invoice-reminder reliability failure: Stripe or your app says the invoice was paid, but the reminder cron still sends follow-ups through Resend, Postmark, or another email provider.

Do not debug it from the email template first. Prove which state is allowed to stop the automation, then replay the exact order of invoice payment, webhook delivery, cron selection, and email-provider events.

The invariant to protect

A paid invoice must be excluded from future reminder sends exactly once. Email delivery, opens, clicks, or bounces can update email logs, but they should not decide invoice payment truth.

Narrow review scope: invoice status, reminder queue, webhook replay, and cron send guard. Usually EUR149 when the app is live or near-launch.

1. Pick the canonical payment state

Invoice reminders go wrong when the app has several almost-true states. Pick one state that stops reminders and treat every other signal as supporting evidence.

  • invoice.paid from Stripe can stop reminders for Stripe-owned invoices.
  • A local invoices.status = paid row can stop reminders when manual payments or uploads are supported.
  • A payment ledger row can stop reminders only if it is tied to the invoice and reconciled to a paid status.
  • Email engagement events should never mark the invoice paid.

2. Separate subscription billing from invoice collection

If the SaaS itself has a Stripe subscription and the customer invoices also have payment state, keep those two money loops separate.

SaaS subscription loop

  • Stripe Checkout creates the customer subscription.
  • Customer Portal changes plan or cancellation state.
  • Subscription webhooks control app access and plan limits.

Invoice collection loop

  • Uploaded or generated invoice moves through sent/viewed/paid/overdue.
  • Reminder cron selects unpaid invoices only.
  • Email-provider webhooks update delivery state, not payment truth.

3. Check the cron selection query

The bug is often a query that selects due reminders from a stale scheduled row instead of joining back to the current invoice status.

-- Example shape only: adapt table names to your app.
select r.id, r.invoice_id, i.status, r.scheduled_for
from scheduled_reminders r
join invoices i on i.id = r.invoice_id
where r.status = 'pending'
  and r.scheduled_for <= now()
  and i.status not in ('paid', 'void', 'archived');
  • If the cron reads only scheduled_reminders.status, paid invoices can keep sending.
  • If payment marks only the invoice row, queued reminders need cancellation or a send-time guard.
  • If retries requeue failed emails, the retry path must also re-check invoice status.

4. Replay the exact failure order

  • Reminder scheduled at T1.
  • Invoice becomes paid at T2 through Stripe, manual mark-paid, or imported payment.
  • Payment webhook or local action updates the invoice row at T3.
  • Cron runs at T4 and either skips or sends the reminder.
  • Email provider webhook returns at T5 and updates only the email log.

5. Regression tests worth keeping

  • Queued reminder is skipped when invoice is paid before the cron run.
  • Failed email retry is skipped if the invoice was paid before retry.
  • Stripe invoice.paid webhook replay does not duplicate payment rows or resend reminders.
  • Manual mark-paid cancels or guards scheduled reminders.
  • Email open/click/delivery webhooks never mark the invoice paid.
  • Public invoice token cannot mutate paid state without the expected payment path.
