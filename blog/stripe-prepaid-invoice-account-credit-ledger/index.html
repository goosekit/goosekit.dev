Prepaid invoices + account credits

Stripe prepaid invoice but account credit ledger drifted?

Prepaid service money is easy to double-count. A customer can hold account credit, an invoice can become prepaid, Stripe autopay may still be collectable, and revenue should be recognized only when the credit is applied to the serviced invoice.

The critical path is not the button that applies credit. It is the ledger invariant: one signed credit movement, one cached balance, one invoice status, and one revenue-recognition event.

The invariant to protect

Issuing account credit should not create revenue. Applying account credit to an invoice should move exactly one amount from credit balance to invoice collection, mark the invoice prepaid, and block a second collection path.

1. Keep credit issuance separate from revenue

Account credit is a holding bucket. The ledger row that issues or adjusts credit should explain the customer balance, not pretend that an invoice has been paid.

Credit truth

  • Append-only `customer_credit_ledger` row
  • Signed delta and `balance_after`
  • Cached `customers.account_credits` value
  • Source such as manual, prepay, adjustment, or later referral

Invoice truth

  • Invoice status `prepaid`, not Stripe-paid
  • `credit_applied` amount and `prepaid_at` timestamp
  • Payment or revenue row created only when applied
  • Public pay page blocked from collecting again

2. Replay the cases that break prepaid billing

  • Credit is issued, but no invoice is serviced yet: balance changes, revenue does not.
  • Full credit covers the invoice: invoice becomes `prepaid`, follow-ups stop, pay page cannot collect.
  • Partial credit is applied: balance due remains collectable and the invoice is not marked fully prepaid.
  • Stripe autopay races manual apply: the system does not collect both card payment and account credit.
  • Referral or promo credit exists elsewhere: it is not double-credited into account credit without reconciliation.

3. Query for stranded or double-counted credit

-- Example shape only.
select
  i.id as invoice_id,
  i.status,
  i.credit_applied,
  c.account_credits,
  l.balance_after
from invoices i
join customers c on c.id = i.customer_id
left join customer_credit_ledger l on l.invoice_id = i.id
where i.status = 'prepaid'
  and (i.credit_applied is null or l.id is null);
  • Find prepaid invoices without a matching credit-ledger row.
  • Find ledger application rows that did not update invoice status.
  • Find invoices marked paid/prepaid twice through credit and Stripe collection.

What this review is not

It is not tax advice, bookkeeping policy, or revenue-recognition consulting. It is a billing-state review: whether the app can prove credit issue, credit application, invoice status, collection blocking, and local revenue rows agree.
