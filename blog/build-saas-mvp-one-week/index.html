The One-Week Sprint Plan

Day 1 — Setup + Core Feature

Deploy Ship It Kit. Configure Stripe and Supabase with your keys. Ship the core feature — the one thing that makes your SaaS worth paying for.

Day 2 — UX + First Users

Polish the onboarding flow. Get 5-10 people to sign up. Use their feedback to cut the worst UX problems before they become assumptions.

Day 3 — Payment Integration

Connect your core feature to Stripe checkout. Set up your pricing page. Start with a single plan — one price, one CTA.

Day 4 — Retention Basics

Add the essential onboarding email, a "getting started" dashboard state, and a way to contact support. Small things that prevent churn.

Day 5 — SEO + Launch Page

Write the landing page copy. Add the essential SEO tags. Publish a launch post on one community (Indie Hackers, Reddit, Twitter). Start collecting signups.

Day 6 — Public Launch

Post to 2-3 communities. Reply to every comment. Collect emails. If people are interested, a few will ask about pricing — that is your first signal.

Day 7 — Iterate + Validate

Talk to the people who signed up. Fix the top 2 complaints. Ship one improvement. Decide: keep building or pivot?

What to Skip in Week One

  • Multi-tenancy — Build for your first 50 users, not for 50,000.
  • Email automation sequences — A single welcome email is enough for now.
  • Admin dashboards — You can read Stripe data directly.
  • Dark mode — Ship in light mode. Add dark mode when someone asks for it.
  • Mobile optimization — Your first users are on desktop. Focus there first.
  • Analytics beyond Stripe — First week, just track signups and revenue.

First Week Success Metrics

Track these, not vanity numbers:
