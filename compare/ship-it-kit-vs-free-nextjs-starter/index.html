Ship It Kit vs free Next.js starters

Free starters are often the right choice. A paid starter only makes sense when it removes a setup loop you were going to rebuild anyway: auth, billing, protected pages, webhooks, and the first dashboard shell.

See Ship It Kit proof →Read the auth + billing tradeoff →
Short version: choose free when you want maximum control and have time to wire the product foundation yourself. Choose Ship It Kit only when Next.js + Supabase + Stripe is already your stack and speed matters more than assembling plumbing from scattered examples.

The honest decision

Choose a free starter if…

  • You enjoy owning every architectural choice.
  • You need a custom auth or billing model.
  • You are learning the stack and time is not the constraint.
  • You do not need checkout or webhooks this week.

Choose Ship It Kit if…

  • You already want Next.js 15, Supabase auth, and Stripe.
  • You are validating a SaaS MVP and need billing early.
  • You want a smaller starter instead of a large framework.
  • You want a repo that is clear enough for AI-assisted editing.

The real cost of “free”

Free is not bad. The hidden cost is context switching: finding examples, checking if they match the App Router, adapting auth callbacks, preserving raw Stripe webhook bodies, mapping customers to users, and then explaining the project structure to your AI coding tool or future self.

If that work teaches you something useful, do it. If it is blocking validation of the actual product, buying a focused base can be rational.

Why Ship It Kit stays small

Ship It Kit is not trying to beat every large SaaS boilerplate on feature count. It is intentionally scoped around the first paid-product foundation: landing page, pricing flow, auth, dashboard shell, Stripe checkout, portal, webhooks, and setup notes.

That smaller scope matters when you are building with Cursor, Claude, Copilot, or by hand: less inherited code means less to understand before you can ship.

Verdict

Start free if you are exploring or learning. Consider Ship It Kit only when you are ready to test a paid SaaS idea and want the Next.js + Supabase + Stripe foundation handled before product work starts.
