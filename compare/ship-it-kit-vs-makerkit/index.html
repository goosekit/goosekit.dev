Which one should you pick?

Choose MakerKit if you want a bigger SaaS framework with teams, billing options (Stripe, Lemon Squeezy, Paddle), email, AI integrations, and a lot of built-in surface area — and you are comfortable onboarding into a larger codebase and paying yearly.

Choose Ship It Kit if you want auth + billing done cleanly on Next.js 15 + Supabase + Stripe, a repo you can read end-to-end in an afternoon, and a focused one-time starter model instead of a yearly subscription.

Ship It Kit is for the stage where signup, checkout, and the billing plumbing are slowing the real product work — not yet a full SaaS OS.

Frequently asked

Is Ship It Kit a MakerKit alternative?
Ship It Kit is an independent starter with a narrower scope — Next.js 15 + Supabase + Stripe specifically, with checkout currently held until price verification. MakerKit is a larger SaaS framework. Ship It Kit is not a fork or a drop-in replacement for MakerKit's full feature set.
What exactly do I get with Ship It Kit?
A clean Next.js 15 starter repo with Supabase email + OAuth + magic links, session-aware app gating, Stripe Checkout, customer portal, webhook handlers, billing settings page, password reset, and a typed database layer. Roughly 5 core files to understand to get the auth + billing flow.
Why is Ship It Kit one-time and MakerKit yearly?
Different philosophies. MakerKit's yearly model funds ongoing framework development across a large surface area. Ship It Kit is a smaller starter you own outright once you buy — with lifetime updates included, no yearly renewal.
Can I use Ship It Kit with an AI coding tool?
Yes. The smaller scope is intentional — it keeps the repo easy for Cursor, Claude, and Copilot to navigate and edit without wading through a large framework.
What if it is the wrong fit after buying?
30-day money-back guarantee, no questions asked. Instant starter download after purchase so you can evaluate it immediately.
