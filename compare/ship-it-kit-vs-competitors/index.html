What this table misses

Features tell one story. Scope and maintenance burden tell another. Here's what the checkboxes don't say:

  • Ship It Kit is smaller by design. It's the auth + billing layer only — not a full SaaS framework. If you want multi-tenant teams, admin panels, and email flows included, you want one of the bigger options. If you want to understand what you have before adding your product logic, Ship It Kit is built for that.
  • ShipFast is the established option. More community discussion, more testimonials, more integrations. It's also the loudest, which means it attracts both the most buyers and the most vocal critics.
  • Makerkit and Supastarter are good mid-weight choices. Stronger on features, heavier on scope. Good if you know you need teams and billing from day one. More to learn on day one too.
  • All the big starters fight AI tooling. They're all large enough that Cursor, Claude, and Copilot slow down or get confused. Ship It Kit has an explicit CLAUDE.md and a deliberately minimal repo structure to stay editable by AI tools.
