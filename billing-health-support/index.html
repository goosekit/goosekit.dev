Billing action queue review

Some teams do not need billing automation to fire more actions. They need billing events to raise the right human-owned card, resolve it at the right time, and leave enough evidence to explain why a customer is in trouble.

What gets checked

  • Stripe events that raise trouble: failed invoice, past-due subscription, unpaid state, or renewal mismatch.
  • Billing ledger row that makes each raised card idempotent and replayable.
  • Action queue row or retention card shown to the human operator.
  • Resolve rule when payment recovers, subscription changes, or the issue is manually completed.

Failure modes

  • Duplicate webhook delivery creates duplicate cards.
  • Out-of-order recovery hides a newer billing problem.
  • Queue write fails but webhook returns success.
  • Score or rank changes but the human-visible card does not explain the money state.

Monthly review output

Each month should leave you with a short practical note, not a vague “looks good”. The review focuses on the few states that usually leak revenue in small Stripe/Supabase apps.

Event check

  • Recent failed webhook deliveries.
  • Duplicate or out-of-order subscription events.
  • Unhandled billing events worth adding.

State check

  • Stripe subscriptions vs Supabase paid rows.
  • Trial, past-due, unpaid, paused, canceled, and portal-change state.
  • Customer/user/workspace mapping drift.

Action check

  • One small fix, test, or logging improvement.
  • One billing action queue or retention-card risk when relevant.
  • One risk to ignore for now.
  • Clear escalation if the issue is bigger than this support scope.

Make the monthly check query-shaped

Monthly support is easiest to buy and repeat when the invariant can be checked with one reconciliation query: Stripe truth on one side, Supabase access state on the other, and a small list of mismatch rows to review.

Bring this

  • Stripe mirror table or event-derived subscription state.
  • Supabase user, team, workspace, entitlement, credit, or booking table.
  • One join key and one policy for active, trialing, past-due, paused, canceled, or unpaid.

Monthly value

  • Rows found become the review queue.
  • No rows found still proves the billing invariant for the month.
  • Repeated mismatch types become a fix/test plan instead of vague monitoring.

Founding-5 intake filter

The first five monthly slots should go only to apps where recurring review can protect real or near-real revenue. A good request includes one Stripe object, one Supabase access table or row, and one monthly state that should be checked.

Ready to review

  • Live or pre-launch checkout, subscription, deposit, booking, or add-on flow.
  • Stripe event, invoice, subscription, customer, or Checkout Session id available without secrets.
  • Supabase table or row that controls paid access, credits, seats, bookings, workspace status, or a billing action queue.

Not ready yet

  • No Stripe Billing flow, webhook endpoint, or paid access state exists yet.
  • The request is broad feature work rather than a billing-state review.
  • There is no concrete monthly invariant to check.

What this is not

This sits above Stripe Billing, dunning tools, webhook delivery tools, and Stripe-to-Supabase sync. Those can be useful. They do not automatically answer whether your app should grant access right now.

Not replacing

  • Dunning email automation or Smart Retries.
  • Webhook queues, replay infrastructure, or event delivery monitoring.
  • Generic Stripe data sync or ETL into Supabase.
  • Broad product development or unlimited support.

Actually checking

  • Whether Stripe billing state and Supabase entitlement state disagree.
  • Whether trials, failed renewals, portal changes, and pauses map to your access policy.
  • Whether a reconciliation query can find silent paid-state drift.
  • Which one small fix would reduce the most revenue risk this month.

Good fit

  • Live or near-live SaaS with paying/trial users.
  • Next.js + Supabase + Stripe or very close.
  • You care about failed renewals, trial conversion, paused billing, portal changes, and paid access state.
  • You want a narrow recurring safety check, not a full-time developer.

Not a fit

  • No deployed app or Stripe Billing setup yet.
  • You need broad feature work or unlimited support.
  • Your billing is not Stripe or your access layer is not close to Supabase.
  • You cannot share enough safe context to review the system.
